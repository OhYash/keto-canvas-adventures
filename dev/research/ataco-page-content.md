# Ataco Page — Raw Content from Owner

Collected: 2026-07-08 (dictated). All bike names confirmed by owner (Yezdi Scrambler,
Hero Xpulse, TVS RTX 300).

## The bike
- Triumph Scrambler 400X, khaki green. First motorcycle.
- Bought 2026-04-06 from the Jaipur showroom; ~1000 km by early July.
- **Last-piece exclusive**: it was the showroom's final 400cc unit before the India
  lineup shifted to 349cc — the 400 is no longer available as a fresh buy.
- Named Ataco. The naming story is deliberately NOT explained on the page — leave it
  as an unexplained fact. Do not invent or hint at a reason.

## The decision (years in the making)
- Overthought the purchase for many years; avid YouTube motorcycle/riding content watcher.
- Shortlist considered and why each fell away:
  - Yezdi Scrambler — after-sales support wasn't there
  - Royal Enfield Scram (411) — (nickel-level issues; power / spoked-tubed class of concerns)
  - Royal Enfield Himalayan
  - Royal Enfield Classic
  - Hero Xpulse
  - TVS RTX 300 — no test ride available, and he will not buy a motorcycle
    without a test ride (firm personal rule)
- Recurring dealbreakers across candidates: not enough power; spoked wheels (= tubed
  tyres); missing after-sales network.

## Ownership so far
- Ridden sparingly, deliberately: no commute use; occasional rides around Alwar (home)
  and visits to Delhi. No trips beyond Delhi yet — bigger rides planned when the
  weather supports it.
- First summer of ownership was brutal: most days the bike just stood parked. "Who
  rides summers in India?" — it gets very, very hot.
- Daytime rides only, as a rule. Good visibility; night rides on two-wheelers are
  scary because an animal can appear from anywhere, any time.
- Hasn't dropped it — but almost did once. Lesson learned: when parking, the
  handlebar must be turned toward the side-stand side; turned the other way the bike
  loses balance. The stand holds it very upright, so there's little margin in the
  parked stance.
