The second analyst caught a critical flaw: your site uses boilerplate elements that scream "AI-generated template tool." To make your digital brain feel real, you must ruthlessly cut the fluff.

* **Remove Stock Photos Immediately:** This is a massive credibility leak. If a visitor goes to a personal section about *your* cat and *your* contact info, and sees an Unsplash stock photo, the illusion of an authentic "digital brain" breaks instantly. ## 4. Solving the "Hidden Section" Navigation Problem

Because the site is a 2D canvas, users don't know that deeper content (like **Travel** under Personal, or **Projects** under Hobbies) even exists.

* **The Fix:** Combine the navigation hints. Implement a strict **Axis Layout** (Professional on the horizontal track, Personal on the vertical track) and use micro-copy at the bottom of parent cards to hint at what lies beyond the border.
* **The Copy Signposts:** At the bottom of the Hobbies card, add a tiny indicator: `[ Projects Deeper Down ↓ ]`. At the bottom of the Work card, add: `[ ← Slide Left for Side Projects ]`.
